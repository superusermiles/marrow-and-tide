# Marrow & Tide — Project Brief

## Business Overview
- **Business name:** Marrow & Tide
- **Location:** 14 Norwich Quay, Lyttelton 8082, Christchurch, New Zealand
- **Tagline:** Coastal suppers. Pantry staples. Slow hospitality.

## What They Do
Marrow & Tide is a chef-led coastal supper club and provisions studio shaped by life at sea and long winters in southern kitchens. By night, the space becomes a candlelit long-table dining room where local catch, smoked vegetables, bone broths, wild herbs, and slow, generous plates are served with the intimacy of a harbour gathering. By day, it shifts into a working pantry for the port community, lined with cultured butter, sea salt blends, pickles, preserves, broths, and fresh loaves that feel made for weathered tables and practical rituals.

This is not a conventional restaurant and not just a retail pantry. It is part galley, part larder, part neighbourhood ritual — grounded in provenance, seasonality, and hospitality that feels deeply local to Lyttelton.

## Target Audience
- Design-conscious Lyttelton locals who care about craft, atmosphere, and where their food comes from
- Weekend visitors coming over the hill from Christchurch for a memorable meal or pantry stop
- Food tourists seeking intimate, destination-worthy dining rooted in New Zealand produce and place
- Couples, small groups, and solo diners drawn to thoughtful hospitality over loud trend-driven venues
- People who buy provisions with intention: cultured staples, preserves, bread, and gifts that feel useful, beautiful, and worth bringing home

## Brand Voice
Warm, tactile, confident, and lightly poetic. Salt-worn and grounded, but quietly refined. Editorial in restraint, human in tone. Marrow & Tide should sound like someone who knows exactly what they are serving and why, without ever becoming stiff, rustic theatre, or faux-luxury.

## Pages Needed
- **Home:** Atmospheric introduction to the brand, dual offering, featured suppers, pantry highlights, and primary booking pathways
- **About:** Founders’ story, harbour-rooted philosophy, sourcing relationships, and the people behind the table
- **Suppers:** Clear explanation of the dining format, upcoming seasonal events, private gathering options, and FAQ details
- **Pantry:** Product categories, featured staples, weekly drop information, and enquiry pathway for provisions
- **Contact:** Address, opening hours, booking/contact details, enquiry form, and location information

## Specific Requirements from the Brief
- Build a distinct editorial-coastal identity with tactile restraint rather than generic hospitality styling
- Keep the layout airy, paced, cinematic, and highly image-led
- Use the provided contrast-safe palette exactly as the design foundation
- Typography must use **Cormorant Garamond** for headings, **Manrope** for body, and **IBM Plex Mono** sparingly for utility text
- Navigation should begin transparent over the hero and become sticky/solid on scroll
- Desktop navigation: left links, centered logo, right-aligned booking CTA
- Hero must be full-bleed with dark overlay, large serif headline, short intro, and dual CTAs
- Footer must use a three-column structure with contact, hours, newsletter/provisions note, and social links
- Include a custom SVG logo system and SVG favicon
- Include OG image direction for a 1200×630 editorial social card
- Motion should stay subtle: fade/slide reveals, gentle image zoom, brass-accent button hover, no flashy animations
- Copy should balance appetite and clarity, with strong locality to Lyttelton harbour and seasonal New Zealand produce
- Include placeholders for booking integration and simple enquiry flow
